Evaluation Rubric for Performance Innovative Task in Parallel and Distributed Computing 
This rubric is used to evaluate your group’s Performance Innovative Task in Parallel and Distributed Computing. The assessment focuses on both your actual system implementation and performance as well as your ability to explain, justify, and document your design decisions. 
The evaluation is divided into two main components: 
● Presentation, System Demonstration, and Stress Testing (70%) 
This measures how effectively your system applies parallel and distributed computing concepts, how well it performs under load, and how clearly your team explains the technical design and implementation. 
● Technical Brochure (30%) 
This evaluates how well your system is documented, including its architecture, applied methodologies, performance evidence, and overall clarity and professionalism of presentation. 
Each criterion is rated on a scale of 1 to 5, where: 
● 5 (Excellent) indicates strong, correct, and well-justified implementation 
● 3 (Satisfactory) indicates acceptable but limited or inconsistent implementation 
● 1 (Poor) indicates minimal, incorrect, or missing implementation 
Your final grade is computed by normalizing your scores per section and applying the corresponding weight, resulting in a total score out of 100.

A. Presentation Flow (10 Minutes Total) 
Each group will be given a total of 10 minutes to present their system. The presentation is expected to be concise, technical, and focused on demonstrating how parallel and distributed computing concepts are applied in your project. The goal is not only to show that your system works, but to clearly explain how it leverages concurrency, distribution, and performance optimization. 
The presentation will follow a fixed structure: 
1. System Explanation & Demonstration (3 minutes) 
During this part, you will briefly introduce your system and demonstrate its core features. Your explanation should focus on how the system operates and where parallel and distributed computing concepts are integrated within the implementation. 
● Brief system overview (purpose and functionality) 
● Demonstration of key features 
● Identification of where PDC concepts are applied (like, threads, processes, workers, APIs)
2. Stress Testing (5 minutes) 
You are required to perform a live stress test to demonstrate how your system behaves under concurrent usage. This is a critical component of the evaluation, as it reflects the effectiveness of your design in handling real-world load conditions. 
● Simulate concurrent users or requests 
● Explain what is being tested (such as number of users, type of workload) 
● Present performance metrics such as response time, throughput, or latency 
● Describe how your system handles scaling, load distribution, or bottlenecks 
3. Question & Answer (2 minutes) 
The final portion of the presentation will assess your understanding of your own system. Panelists may ask about your architecture, implementation decisions, and the specific parallel or distributed techniques used. 
All members are expected to be prepared to answer questions and demonstrate familiarity with the system. B. Technical Brochure Requirements 
Each group must prepare a formal brochure (PDF format) that will be reviewed by the panelists prior to the presentation. The brochure should clearly communicate your system’s design, features, and technical foundation in a structured and professional manner. It should serve as a concise but informative reference document that aligns with your actual implementation. 
The brochure must include the following components: 
1. System Overview 
Provide a brief description of your system, including its purpose, target users, and general functionality. 
● System title 
● Short description of the proposed and developed system
2. Team Information 
Identify the members involved in the project. You may optionally include roles to clarify contributions. 
● Names of all members 
● (Optional) Roles or responsibilities 
3. System Features 
Describe the key functionalities of your system. This section should be supported by visuals to help illustrate how the system works in practice. 
● List of major features 
● Screenshots or interface visuals 
● Short explanation for each feature 
4. Architecture & Design 
Present a diagram that reflects the structure of your system. This should clearly show how components interact and how data flows within the system. 
● Nodes, workers, servers, or components 
● Communication flow and data movement 
5. PDC Concepts Applied 
Explain the parallel and distributed computing concepts used in your system. This section should go beyond naming tools and instead focus on their role in the design. 
● Concepts used (like multithreading, multiprocessing, client-server, load balancing) 
● Where these concepts are applied 
● Why they are appropriate for your system
6. Performance Evidence 
Provide results from your system testing to support your claims about performance and scalability. Data should be clearly presented and easy to interpret. 
● Metrics (such as response time, throughput) 
● Comparisons (such as idle vs stress, before vs after) 
● Graphs, tables, or summarized results 
We will discuss this in class after the final lecture or I will provide a document for you to review. 7. Design Rationale 
Explain the reasoning behind your chosen system and design decisions. This should reflect your understanding of both the problem and the solution approach. 
● Why this system was selected 
● Justification of design and implementation choices 
8. Visual Quality 
The brochure should be well-organized, readable, and professionally formatted. Clarity and structure are important to ensure that panelists can easily understand your work. 
● Clean and organized layout 
● Balanced use of text and visuals 
● Readable formatting 
The brochure and presentation must be consistent with each other and must accurately reflect your actual system implementation. Avoid including unsupported claims or fabricated data. Your work will be evaluated not only on functionality, but also on how well you justify and communicate your technical decisions.
